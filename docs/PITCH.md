# Rapport Pitch

## One-Liner

Rapport is a HydraDB-powered desktop relationship intelligence app that turns meetings, emails, and calendar context into live pre-call briefs and a living relationship graph.

## The Problem

Professional relationships are full of important signals that current tools lose:

- A buyer quietly shifts from champion to skeptic.
- A technical evaluator gains influence over the deal.
- A budget owner blocks progress without appearing in every meeting.
- The same concern appears across three emails and two calls.
- A commitment is made verbally and never reaches the CRM.

CRMs store fields. Notes store fragments. Chat tools forget the broader arc. Rapport gives the user a memory layer for the relationship itself.

## The Product

Rapport is a floating desktop overlay for high-context professional work.

Before a call, it briefs the user:

- current stance
- recent changes
- top concerns
- communication preferences
- political influence map
- talking points
- landmines to avoid

During a call, it listens and captures signals.

After a call, it updates memory and relationship context.

Over time, it builds a graph of people, topics, commitments, stances, and influence.

## Why HydraDB Is The Hero

This product only works if memory is durable, searchable, scoped, and semantically useful. HydraDB gives Rapport the core primitives:

- **Memories** capture the behavioral observations that make each relationship feel personal.
- **Knowledge** captures the factual context from emails and transcripts.
- **Recall** retrieves the right context at the moment of action.
- **Sub-tenants** isolate contact-level memory without fragmenting the whole workspace.
- **Graph-oriented context** maps influence and relationships, not just text chunks.

Rapport is intentionally designed as a HydraDB-native product. The UX exists to reveal the value of HydraDB memory in a daily workflow.

## What Makes It Compelling

Rapport is not another meeting recorder. It is a relationship operating layer.

The differentiator is not transcription. The differentiator is accumulated judgment:

- "Mira prefers written summaries."
- "Priya controls technical approval."
- "Owen is blocking budget until procurement resolves timing."
- "Security review has appeared in the last three interactions."
- "Do not pitch this as a lightweight pilot."

Those are the signals that change outcomes.

## Demo Narrative

1. Open Rapport as a floating desktop overlay.
2. Show the current contact and relationship graph.
3. Trigger a pre-call brief.
4. Start live capture.
5. Watch transcript snippets flow into the overlay.
6. Stop capture and explain how the interaction becomes HydraDB memory.
7. Show how the next brief is improved by recall.
